The Big Picture

Why we're building this

Our company is a vendor that supports multiple client companies. Instead of paying recurring per-agent fees, we build our own service desk — on a platform that hands us auth, roles and reporting for free.

💸 Kill the licence cost

Tools like Jira / Zendesk charge per agent, forever. We build once and own it.

🧩 APEX is the right fit

Built-in login, role-based access control and charts. We build with the platform, not against it.

🏆 A real pitch for judges

Genuine business value, multi-tenant architecture, production-grade story — not a toy app.

Roles & Access

Four roles, two sides

The client side (our customers) and the vendor side (our company). Multi-company isolation lives in the jump from Client Admin (one company) to System Admin (all companies).

Client side

Client User

SeesOnly their own tickets
DoesRaise a ticket, comment, track status, assign agent (from mapped agents)
Client side

Client Admin

SeesAll of their company's tickets
DoesOversee staff, raise on behalf, company dashboard, assign/reassign agents
Our side

Support Agent

SeesTickets for their assigned projects (clients they cover) — never clients they're not on
DoesWork tickets: status, comment, resolve
Our side

System Admin

SeesEverything, across all companies
DoesManage companies/users, assign, configure
👥
Four roles are enough (decision I). The things you'd expect to be extra roles aren't: a Manager who only watches is just an Admin/overseer who doesn't click the work buttons; L1/L2/L3 tiers are the Escalate action (reassign + raise priority, logged) — no per-agent level field; a Project Lead is deferred (returns later as an is_lead flag, not a role). Agents are scoped to their assigned projects via the AGENT_COMPANIES table below.

🛡️
Tenant isolation plan (v1): at login, store the user's company_id + role in an APEX application item. Every client-facing report filters WHERE company_id = :APP_COMPANY_ID, with APEX authorization schemes layered on top. In the demo, show a client logging in and seeing only their tickets — that proves it live. Agents are additionally narrowed to their own projects via WHERE company_id IN (their AGENT_COMPANIES) — a usability filter on the vendor side, separate from the hard client-isolation wall.

Keep it small

Things that are deliberately NOT pages

Status transitions & Escalate

New→Assigned→In Progress→On Hold→Resolved→Closed→Reopen, plus Escalate (FR-26) = buttons + processes on Ticket Detail, each writing a TICKET_HISTORY row. Zero extra pages.

Comments & History

Regions embedded inside Ticket Detail (page 5), not standalone pages.

Navigation, theme & branding

Menu, breadcrumbs and the branded look are Shared Components — not pages.

Emails (FR-21, FR-29)

Assignment + auto-acknowledgement emails are APEX_MAIL processes on the Assign / Create actions — not pages.

CSAT rating (FR-27)

A native Star Rating item on Ticket Detail, shown when status = Closed — not a page.
